# Executed bans — 2026-05-25 (FP-43631 follow-up week 3)

Surgical ban of four players hand-confirmed as rating-drop abusers. Unlike weeks 1–2 (HAVING-derived
no-show cohort), this week's list came from **labelling each competition in the Mongo Tournament log
as PLAYED vs NO-SHOW** (`started scoring time` present/absent). The discriminator: these players post
a strong positive when they actually play (they are TOPS-caliber) yet no-show ~half their registrations,
bleeding off the gains to pin PCR in the low (NOOBS/MIDDLES) bracket where they win prizes.

Full evidence and per-player ledgers: `pcr-log-trajectories-2026-05-25.md`.

## Columns
- **Played:** competitions actually started / sum of CompetitionRating gained on them
- **No-show:** competitions registered-but-not-started / sum of penalty on them (raw delta; slightly
  overcounts the PCR move because PCR clamps at 0)
- **No-show %:** share of registrations that were no-shows
- **PCR:** `Profiles.CompetitionRating` at ban time

### Incident — Xbox leaderboard sync not committed
First verification showed VITAO4460 and profpaulo18 with `CompetitiveRatingsCurrent.IsBanned=0` on all
periods while Steam/PS were correctly `IsBanned=1`. Cause: `leaderboard-ban-sync.sql` was run on Xbox PROD
but the transaction was **not committed** (recurring — happened on Xbox before). Fixed with a targeted
`UPDATE … SET IsBanned=1 WHERE UserId IN (…2 Xbox ids…) AND IsBanned=0`, committed; re-verified all true.
**Lesson:** the competition ban and the LB sync are separate transactions — after every ban, verify
`CompetitiveRatingsCurrent.IsBanned` per platform individually, Xbox especially.
